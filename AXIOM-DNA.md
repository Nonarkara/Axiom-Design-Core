## 1. THE CREDO

Front matter. Page one of anything. The whole system in six lines.

> Beauty is not flash. Beauty is what remains after everything that does not work is gone.
>
> Function comes first: it earns the right to be looked at. Then subtraction begins, and subtraction is where beauty lives.
>
> Good design is not understood. It is absorbed: the body acts before the mind explains. A sign you read twice has failed.
>
> Every surviving element carries three loads — it informs, it moves, it means. Drop one and it is decoration. Decoration is a lie about who we are.
>
> The boldest move, purely cut, stops looking bold. It looks inevitable: as if it had always been done this way, though no one had. The circle too big for its rectangle is not daring. It is the size a circle should be.
>
> From the street, we look monotonous. The essence is inside.

### 1.1 The Equation

**MoMA Law × Golden Section × The Divine Move = Axiom.**

Planar discipline, times proportion, times one humanistic rule-break. Discipline alone is the morgue. The spike is what breathes. Remove the spike and the work is correct and dead. Add a second spike and the work is noise.

### 1.2 The Tenets

Each one earned in conversation. Each one a rule, not a mood.

- **Subtraction Is the Beauty.** Working does not become beautiful. *Stripping* the working does. A broken thing simplified is a tidier broken thing. Cut after it works, down to the part that reaches the body before the mind.
- **Pre-Cognitive Action.** The body acts before the mind explains. Design for absorption, not memorization. A sign read twice has already failed.
- **The Triple Load.** Every surviving element informs, *moves*, and *means*. If it carries fewer than three loads, it is ornament. Delete it.
- **Boldness Become Ordinary.** The Divine Move is the gesture the function already demanded — executed full-size in pure form until it looks inevitable. The driver circle too big for its rectangle. Nobody built it before; everybody feels they always had.
- **Essence in the Shadows.** The excitement lives in the light, the section, the vista, the one Move that slips through. Not the dome. The room beneath it.
- **The Categorical Imperative.** An axiom is a self-evident first principle. Test every pattern: would you will it into universal law? If it works only here, it is not an axiom. Delete it.
- **The Lined Glass.** Feedback you read against printed graduations beats feedback you measure from above. Zero latency. Every surface graduates its own state. You never look twice to know where you stand.
- **The Ongard Move.** Steal and improve. Take the best, elevate it, make it unmistakably yours. Synthesis is origination. This document is the method applied to itself.
- **The Sato Standard.** One material, total constraint, a recognizable voice. Edges softened only where a stressed human needs calming — never for prettiness, only for mercy.
- **Structure First.** Form from function, ornament from within structure. Aesthetics are never applied. They are excavated.

## 2. THE MINDS

Five minds build the method. A lineage of builders and two writers set the body and the voice. When rules conflict, return here: intent outranks the letter.

### 2.1 The Method (five minds)

- **Dieter Rams — restraint, and the bold move inside it.** Less, but better. The greyscale field, the hairline grid, the ban on decoration. And the proof that restraint is not boredom: the driver circle sized to what a speaker *should* be, the player that hangs on the wall like it levitates. Rams is bold. The boldness disappears into rightness.
- **Bill Moggridge — the human at the center.** Before any pixel: who is the human, what is their context, what decision must they make in the next 30 seconds. Prototype as exploration. The interface exists to close the gap between intention and action.
- **Don Norman — the psychology.** Discoverability, feedback, conceptual model, affordance, signifier, mapping, constraint. There is no user error — only design failure. If many people make the same mistake, the system is wrong.
- **MoMA Digital — the structure of culture.** Never touch the art; we never touch the data. Baseline alignment as religion. Modular components. Typographically-derived spacing. Best foot forward at every breakpoint.
- **John Maeda — the poetics of reduction.** Subtract the obvious, add the meaningful. SHE: Shrink, Hide, Embody when you cannot remove. Time saved feels like simplicity. Trust comes from consistency.
- **Vignelli / NYCTA — orientation under load.** Information at the point of decision, never before, never after. Color enclosed for identity, bare for signal. Standardized boldness, because a crowd underground needs bold to find its way.

### 2.2 The Body (the architects)

Structure first. The discipline of an architect: nothing decorative survives that the structure did not ask for.

- **Sullivan → Wright:** form follows function; ornament grows from within structure, not onto it.
- **Louis Kahn:** material honesty, weight, light as a building material. What does the brick want to be. Servant and served, made legible.
- **Early Koolhaas:** the bold programmatic gamble. The conceptual Move at building scale. Bigness that still resolves.
- **Murcutt · Bawa:** touch the earth lightly; answer the climate and the place; vernacular intelligence married to modern clarity. Do not impose — respond.
- **Ongard Satrabundhu:** steal and improve. The Thai master who takes the best of a lineage and elevates it. The patron saint of this document.

### 2.3 The Voice (the writers)

- **Hemingway:** the reporter who paid by the word. Economy as truth. Direct, concrete, and still narrative — directness builds the labyrinth and lands the twist.
- **Bukowski:** plainness with a pulse. No pretension, no costume. Smart by being clear, never by being complicated.

Full voice law in §15.

## 4. THE THREE MODES

One spine. Three registers. Pick the mode by the human's *act*, not by taste.

| | INSTRUMENT | EDITORIAL | PLAY |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The human is** | operating | reading / thinking | learning / playing |
| **Artifacts** | dashboards, systems, infographics, live tools | documents, CVs, essays, reports, decks-as-argument, novels | board/card/flashcard games, workbooks, kits |
| **Type** | Inter, neo-grotesque only | Spectral serif permitted, Inter for labels | either, set for tactility |
| **Color** | greyscale + blue identity + one red spike | greyscale + one accent | trunk subsystem permitted for piece identity |
| **Corners** | square (0–2px) | square | **Sato mercy-radius permitted** |
| **Density** | maximum | reading measure (60–72ch) | generous, hand-scale |
| **Warmth** | none — cold, exact | permitted | encouraged where it invites |
| **Motion** | feedback + pulse only | none | reward feedback permitted |

**Shared spine — never diverges, any mode:** MoMA Law (everything snaps), Golden Section (proportion), Subtraction (cut to function), the Triple Load, honesty over polish, the voice, exactly one Divine Move per surface.

**Mode selection rule:** if the human will *act on data*, Instrument. If they will *read an argument*, Editorial. If they will *learn through their hands*, Play. When unsure, Instrument is the default — it is the most disciplined and the hardest to cheat.

| `--red` | `#A8322B` | **The Move.** Live, critical, decision, negative. Bare. |

> **Warmth rule:** neutrals are warm (a trace of yellow/red), never cool blue-grey. Pure black and pure white are banned.
>
> **The exception rule (Rams + Vignelli):** color appears only at the exception. Normal is grey. Identity is blue. The one spike is red. If a thing is *fine*, it stays grey — the absence of red is the good news.

### 5.2 The Two-Color Law (enclosure disambiguates)

- **Blue, enclosed** (disc, plate, rule) = identity. *Which board am I in? Who are we?* Constant.
- **Red, bare** (text, bar, dot) = signal. *Is this live, critical, decided, down?* Dynamic, rare.
- **Enclosure tells them apart.** A blue disc is identity; a bare red number is signal. This is the grammar that keeps color legible. Never invert it.

### 5.3 The Neutral Ramp (charts, stacked bars)

Three to five categories step through neutrals, never hues:

```
--ink (#191712) → --blue (#26243F) → #8f8b80 → #bdb9ad → #d8d4ca
```

### 5.4 The Trunk Subsystem (NYCTA — Play mode and multi-board dashboards only)

The eight Vignelli/MTA trunk colors survive as a *subsystem*, not the brand. Use only when you genuinely have 5+ parallel boards or game pieces that need family coding. Never as decoration on the main identity.

## 6. TYPOGRAPHY

### 6.1 Families

**Instrument / UI — Inter** (neo-grotesque, Helvetica lineage; the Mondaine and NYCTA voice; free, runs everywhere including PowerPoint, Gamma, email, print).

```css
font-family: 'Inter', 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;
font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums;
-webkit-font-smoothing: antialiased;
```

**Editorial / Reading — Spectral** (a serif that reads long and sits in the New Yorker tradition; free, Google Fonts).

```css
font-family: 'Spectral', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;
```

**Premium tier (when licensing is available):** Helvetica Now (instrument) and a high-contrast Didone for editorial display. The free pair is the default so the system runs anywhere — that is the point.

**Platform fallbacks:** PowerPoint / Google Slides — embed Inter and Spectral if the platform allows; else Arial/Helvetica and Georgia. Email — system stack. Never substitute a rounded or display face.

### 6.2 Type Scale (do not improvise)

| Role | Size px | Weight | Tracking | Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero metric / page number | 30–38 | 600 | -0.01em | — |
| Section metric / big value | 19–24 | 600 | 0 | — |
| Stat value (in a cell) | 15–20 | 600 | 0 | — |
| Body / row value | 12–14 | 500–600 | 0 | sentence |
| Section header (label) | 11 | 700 | 0.16em | UPPERCASE |
| Control / nav | 10–11 | 600 | 0.11em | UPPERCASE |
| Micro-label / meta | 9 | 600 | 0.14em | UPPERCASE, `--ink-3` |

Editorial body overrides this: Spectral, 17–19px, line-height 1.5–1.6, measure 60–72ch.

### 6.3 Rules

- **Weight ceiling 600 for data.** Big numbers are 600, never 700/800. Restraint reads as confidence; heavy weights read as panic.
- **The smaller the text, the wider the tracking.** 9px labels get 0.14–0.18em; body gets 0. This is what makes micro-labels read as engineered.
- **Labels are SMALL, LETTERSPACED, UPPERCASE, GREY.** A label is never the size or color of its value.
- **One label, one value.** No redundant units, no stacked adjectives.
- **Boldness is contrast, not heaviness.** The drama comes from the *gap* between the biggest thing and the finest print — not from making everything heavy. Make the hero very large and the meta very small. The space between them is the boldness.
- **Baseline alignment** across columns and sizes. Flex with `align-items: baseline`.

## 7. GOLDEN SECTION & PROPORTION

φ = 1.618. Find it in every composition. The grid gives alignment; the Golden Section gives proportion and drama.

### 7.1 Where to find it

- **Layout split:** primary/secondary columns at **61.8 / 38.2**. A sidebar, a caption column, a hero/detail split — divide on φ before you divide in half. Halves are static; φ is alive.
- **The page:** major margin to minor margin in φ ratio. Content block to whitespace in φ.
- **The mark:** the logotype, the disc, the relationship of glyph to field — constructed on a φ grid (§9).
- **Cards / panels:** card proportion 1 : 1.618 where the format allows.
- **Vertical rhythm:** the jump from one type size to the next can ride a φ-influenced modular scale.

### 7.2 The collision rule

> When MoMA Law (the snap) and the Golden Section (the proportion) collide with one touch of folly (the Move), great design appears.

The grid keeps you honest. φ keeps you alive. The Move keeps you human. Use all three or you have two-thirds of a design.

## 8. MoMA LAW — GRID, SPACE & PLANAR ALIGNMENT

### 8.1 MoMA Law (the snap)

> **Every edge resolves to another edge. No orphan boundaries.** Trace any edge across the page: before it terminates, it meets a module line, a shared margin, or the edge of a sibling plane — never empty space. Everything snaps to the invisible grid; the grid is invisible *because* everything snaps. If an edge floats free, the layout is unfinished. Order is the absence of accident.

This is the law you can see in a clean MoMA page and never name: nothing is approximately aligned. Everything is *exactly* aligned, to something, on purpose.

### 8.2 Spatial System

```
Container max-width : 1360px (wide tool) · 960px (app) · 720px (reading/doc)
Outer padding       : 22px
Section gap         : 22–24px
Cell padding        : 12–16px
Row vertical padding : 6–13px (density-dependent)
Base unit           : ~11px and its multiples — nothing at an arbitrary offset
```

### 8.3 The Signature Move — Hairline Cell Grids

A grid whose **background is the line color**, `gap:1px`, white cells, renders a precise instrument panel for free.

```html
<div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:repeat(4,1fr);
            gap:1px; background:var(--line); border:1px solid var(--line);">
  <div style="background:var(--panel); padding:13px 16px;"> …cell… </div>
</div>
```

### 8.4 Layout Discipline

- Left-align labels, right-align numbers. Always.
- Flex/grid with `gap` for siblings — never bare inline-block + margins.
- Whitespace is structural, not cosmetic. It groups, separates, signals.
- A faint meta line (9–10px `--ink-3`) under data carries provenance, date, caveat. Honesty over polish.

### 8.5 The Cockpit Pattern (live tools)

Keep the fewest instruments needed to operate safely permanently in view; everything else is one decision away, never sooner.

```html
<div style="position:sticky; top:0; z-index:50;
            background:var(--paper); border-bottom:1px solid var(--line-2);">
  <!-- masthead · navigation · vital-signs strip -->
</div>
```

### 8.6 Breakpoints

Check every one. No "good enough at this width."

```
320 · 375 · 414 · 480 · 600 · 768 · 834 · 900 · 1024 · 1200 · 1360 · 1440 · 1680 · 1920
```

## 9. THE MARK — LOGO SYSTEM

A logo system, not one frozen logo. It generates correct marks for Axiom and its sub-efforts from a fixed grammar.

### 9.1 The Concept

Axiom = a self-evident first principle. The mark must read as a *given*: stable, inevitable, geometrically pure. Two pure forms in honest combination — the Rams lesson: when two pure geometries meet because the function asks, we accept it as something that always existed.

### 9.2 The Grammar

- **The wordmark:** `AXIOM` set in Inter, weight 600, tracking tuned per size, lowercase-height optical balance. Square corners. No ligature theatrics.
- **The Move, built in:** one red element — a single red disc or a red counter inside the `O`/`A` — the Mondaine second-hand idea. One red dot in an otherwise calm mark. The spike is the brand.
- **The disc atom:** a perfect blue disc with a white glyph is the identity unit (see §11). The black/ink hub disc is "home."
- **Construction:** built on a φ grid. Disc diameter to cap-height in φ. Wordmark length to mark height in φ. Clear space = one disc-diameter on every side.

### 9.3 Lockups

- **Primary:** disc + wordmark, horizontal, baseline-aligned.
- **Stacked:** disc above wordmark, centered on the disc's vertical axis (the one sanctioned centering — a mark, not content).
- **Mark-only:** the disc alone, for favicons, avatars, app icons.

### 9.4 Sizes & Reproduction

- **Minimum wordmark width:** 88px screen / 22mm print.
- **Favicon / avatar:** disc-only, glyph legible at 16px.
- **One color:** ink on paper, or paper on ink. The red is the only color that may appear; if one-color is required, the red becomes ink.
- **Never:** stretch, rotate, gradient, outline, drop-shadow, add a tagline inside the clear space, or place on a busy field. The mark sits on silence.

### 9.5 The Lesson Built In

Every year, every serious company's logo gets simpler. They are removing what no longer represents a lean, agile, honest company. Build the Axiom mark already arrived at that endpoint: nothing left to remove.

Flat track, solid fill, square ends. No radius, no gradient. The fill against the track *is* the graduation — state read at a glance.

```html
</div>
```

### 10.5 Stacked Allocation Bar

```html

  <div style="width:38%; background:var(--ink);"></div>
  <div style="width:27%; background:var(--blue);"></div>
  <div style="width:15%; background:#8f8b80;"></div>
</div>
```

### 10.6 Status Plate

```html
<div style="display:flex; align-items:stretch; border:1px solid var(--line-2); background:var(--panel);">
  <div style="background:var(--ink); color:var(--paper); padding:14px 20px; display:flex; align-items:center;">
    <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.18em; font-weight:700;">STATE</span>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:11px 20px; flex:1;"> headline + sub </div>
</div>
```

### 10.7 Buttons

- **Primary:** `background:var(--ink)` or `var(--blue)`; `color:#fff`; padding `10–14px 18–22px`; `font:11–12px/700`, `letter-spacing:0.12em`, UPPERCASE; no radius; hover → `opacity:0.9`.
- **Critical / decision:** `background:var(--red)`. Reserved for the one action that matters on the surface.
- **Tertiary / link:** transparent, `color:var(--ink-2)`, hover → `var(--blue)`. Pair with directional triangle (§11.5).

### 10.8 Inputs

```html
<input type="range" style="width:100%; accent-color:var(--red); height:3px;" />
```
Text fields: `border:1px solid var(--line-2); padding:11px 13px;` — flat, square, no inner shadow, focus = border darken only.

### 10.9 Live Indicator (the red dot)

```html
<span style="width:6px; height:6px; border-radius:50%; background:var(--red);
             display:inline-block; animation:blink 2.4s ease-in-out infinite;"></span>
```
```css
@keyframes blink { 0%,100%{opacity:1} 50%{opacity:.2} }
```
The only sanctioned animation: a slow pulse. The Mondaine second hand of the system.

## 11. WAYFINDING — THE DISC SUBSYSTEM

### 11.1 The Information Tree

> *"Information at the point of decision. Never before. Never after."* — Unimark, 1970

Surface navigation only where a choice is made. One decision, one cue. Branch, don't dump: domain → board → row → detail.

### 11.2 The Disc (identity atom)

Perfect circle, solid trunk or `--blue`, white glyph, no border, no shadow.

```html
<span style="width:34px; height:34px; border-radius:50%; flex:none;
             display:inline-flex; align-items:center; justify-content:center;
             font-size:16px; font-weight:700; color:#fff; background:var(--blue);">A</span>
```

| Context | Diameter | Glyph px |
|---|---|---|
| Nav / inline / legend | 18–20 | 10 |
| Board header / station plate | 32–36 | 15–16 |
| Hero / cover | 56–72 | 26–32 |

Always a true circle. Always a glyph inside. Never two glyphs, never stroked, never gradiented.

### 11.4 The Hub Plate

Masthead / home uses the ink (black) disc — the "hub station."

### 11.5 The Arrow

Solid equilateral triangle. Means *go this way / there is more.* No Unicode arrows, no chevrons.

```html
<span style="display:inline-block; width:0; height:0;
             border-top:4px solid transparent; border-bottom:4px solid transparent;
```

### 11.6 "Should this be colored?"

```
Is it DATA (live / critical / down)?  → bare --red. No disc.            ▸ done
Is it IDENTITY (which board / who)?   → enclose it. Blue disc or rule.  ▸ done
Is it DIRECTIONAL (go here / more)?   → greyscale triangle.             ▸ done
None of these?                        → no color. Grey + size.          ▸ done
```

If you add color "to liven it up," you have failed every master. Delete it.

## 12. INTERACTION & FEEDBACK — THE LINED GLASS

### 12.1 The Lined Glass Principle

Opaque graduated glass beats solid glass: you read the water against the lines, instantly, with no measuring from above. Every Axiom surface graduates its own state. The operator never looks twice to know where they stand.

### 12.2 Norman's Seven, as build rules

| Principle | Build rule | Test |
|---|---|---|
| Discoverability | Every control visible. No hidden gestures. | New user finds the primary action in 10s? |
| Feedback | Visible reaction within 100ms. | Every click has an immediate response? |
| Conceptual model | Structure matches the operator's mental model. Domain → Board → Detail. | User explains the structure after 1 min? |
| Affordances | Buttons look pressable, links followable, data readable. | User guesses function without labels? |
| Signifiers | Labels explicit. Arrow = go. Disc = here. Red = act. | All actions clearly indicated? |
| Mapping | Controls near their effects. Layout matches logic. | Control layout matches data layout? |
| Constraints | Invalid actions disabled. Danger needs confirmation. | One-click catastrophe possible? No. |

### 12.3 The 100ms Rule

Button → state change. Load → indicator. Save → confirmation. Error → clear path. Live → the red pulse, never silence.

### 12.4 Error Prevention > Recovery

Prevent through constraints, not warnings. Disable the impossible rather than error-message it. When many people make the same error, fix the system. Never the words "user error" — say "design opportunity."

### 12.5 Consistency = Learnability

Same action, same way, everywhere. Same pattern, same meaning, everywhere. Learned once, never relearned.

## 13. MOTION

**Allowed:** state changes ≤150ms ease; the slow red pulse; a value counting to its number; feedback within 100ms.

**Banned:** entrance animations, scroll-triggered reveals, parallax, decorative loops, bouncing eases, anything that delays comprehension.

**The rule:** motion confirms, never entertains. If an animation does not answer "did my action work?", it does not belong. (Play mode earns a narrow exception: reward feedback on a correct move — still fast, still purposeful.)

## 14. THE DIVINE MOVE — DOCTRINE OF THE ONE BOLD GESTURE

The antidote to monotony. The reason Axiom is alive and not merely correct.

### 14.1 What it is

Lee Sedol's Move 78: the stroke the machine rated near-impossible, the one a human plays anyway and wins with. In a system this disciplined, you earn **exactly one** bold, slightly-irrational, deeply human gesture per surface.

### 14.2 The hard constraint

- **It must be function-compelled.** Trace it to a function or it is not a Move — it is decoration, and decoration is a lie. The driver circle is too big *because that is the size a speaker should be.*
- **Pure form, full size.** Half a bold move is a timid mistake. Commit. The boldness disappears into rightness.
- **One per surface. Never two.** One is genius. Two is noise. The rarity is the power.
- **It looks inevitable, not daring.** If viewers think "how bold," you overshot. If they think "of course," you landed it.

### 14.3 Where it lives

The oversized hero metric. The single red counter in the mark. The one full-bleed plane in a gridded page. The φ split that breaks the expected symmetry. The room beneath the dome. Essence in the shadows.

## 15. VOICE & LANGUAGE

Hemingway and Bukowski. The reporter who paid by the word, and the plainspeaker with a pulse. We sound smart by being clear — never by being complicated. Complexity worn as a costume for intelligence is the enemy of this whole theory.

### 15.1 The Law

- **Direct.** Conclusion first. Say the thing, then support it.
- **True.** Concrete over abstract. A number over an adjective. "14-day deploy," not "fast." "21 systems, 5 countries," not "many."
- **Economical.** Cut every word that carries no freight, as if you paid by the word to send it over the wire. If a sentence survives the cut, it earned its place.
- **Active.** "We built," not "was built by."
- **Short.** One idea per sentence. Under 20 words where you can.
- **Unpretentious.** No academic hedging, no throat-clearing, no jargon as plumage. Plain words, exactly placed.
- **Narrative, still.** Directness is not boredom. Build the labyrinth of inquiry, then land the twist that gets the reader off the seat. Direct *and* alive.
- **No exclamation marks.** The data is exciting enough. **No emoji.** The red pulse is the only punctuation of aliveness.

### 15.2 Examples

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "We are a leading provider of innovative smart-city solutions" | "21 systems in production. 5 countries. Problem mapped in week one." |
| "Our cutting-edge platform leverages AI and big data" | "47 live data feeds. One surface. 30 seconds to comprehension." |
| "It could be argued that, in a certain sense, the system tends to improve outcomes" | "The system cut the backlog 40%. Here is the chart." |
| "We are excited to announce our upcoming expansion" | "Taipei. March 2026. Live dashboard, demo'd in 45 minutes." |

### 15.3 The Twist

One line per piece may break the rhythm to land the point. Short, sharp, earned. The verbal Divine Move. Use it once. Never twice.

## 16. DENSITY & DATA

"Less, but better" ≠ "less data." It means **less noise.** Keep the data; remove the disorder.

- Group dense data into hairline cell grids and **label every cluster.**
- Right-align numbers; fixed-width figure columns; tabular figures.
- Demote everything that is not the answer to `--ink-2` / `--ink-3`.
- One thousand no's for every yes: no decorative stat, no vanity metric, no icon that doesn't aid a decision.
- **Never touch the data.** No cropping that hides meaning. No chart junk that distorts truth. The data is the art; the interface is the frame.
- **Honesty over polish:** show provenance, dates, delay, uncertainty.

## 17. ICONOGRAPHY, ORNAMENT & THE SATO EXCEPTION

- Prefer a **word** to an icon; prefer a **number** to a word.
- Icons only where they out-perform text: direction, play/pause, close.
- If used: single-weight line or solid, geometric, on the baseline, sized to text. No duotone, no rounded "friendly" sets, no emoji.
- **Ornament is banned** — but ornament is not the same as the Divine Move. The Move is function-compelled and earns its place. Ornament is applied and lies.

### 17.1 The Sato Exception (the mercy-radius)

Shuetsu Sato softened his sign edges for one reason: to calm irritated commuters. That is the only license to break the no-radius ban. A radius is permitted **where it does measurable work on a human under stress or invitation** — Play-mode game pieces a child handles, a wayfinding sign for an anxious crowd, a touch target that must feel safe. Never for prettiness. Always for mercy. Document the reason or remove the radius.

### 17.2 The Xiaohei Exception — Sanctioned Illustration

> "Cute and easy to understand modernist illustration." — Non Arkaraprasertkul

**Xiaohei** ([helloianneo/ian-xiaohei-illustrations](https://github.com/helloianneo/ian-xiaohei-illustrations)) is the one sanctioned illustration system in the Axiom universe. It is the Sato Exception applied to imagery: used where warmth, explanation, or invitation does measurable human work — never for decoration.

**Where Xiaohei belongs:**

- **Onboarding** — first contact with a complex system, when explanation needs a face
- **Empty states** — dashboard with no data yet; the silence needs a human presence, not a grey placeholder
- **Error states** — system failed; warmth reduces friction where a cold error message increases it
- **Play mode** — game pieces, workbooks, flashcard kits where warmth is the mechanism (§4, §18.6)
- **Explanatory panels** — sidebar callout where a diagram would be colder than a character

**Where Xiaohei does NOT belong:**

- Hero sections of Instrument-mode dashboards — they stay cold and exact
- Data cells, rows, or charts — data is never decorated
- Any surface where the Sato condition (stress, invitation, explanation) does not apply
- As a substitute for clear affordance or legibility

**Grammar:**

- **One character or scene per surface.** The Divine Move rule applies to illustration as it does to everything else.
- Never alongside another illustration style on the same surface.
- Never as a background or full-bleed visual.
- **Scale:** small enough to not compete with data; large enough to be absorbed in one glance.
- **Color:** keep the character's own palette intact. Do not recolor to match Axiom tokens. The contrast between the illustration's warmth and the mono field is the point — they are meant to be different materials.
- **The only clickable chip** in a stack or tag cloud where Xiaohei appears is the Xiaohei chip itself — it links to the source. This is the easter egg.

**Credit:** Ian Neo · [ian-xiaohei-illustrations](https://github.com/helloianneo/ian-xiaohei-illustrations). Link to the source wherever the illustration appears.

## 18. CROSS-MEDIA APPLICATION

How each agent applies this DNA. Throw the document; read your section.

### 18.1 Slide / PowerPoint / Gamma Agent → Editorial mode

- One idea per slide. The slide is an argument step, not a bucket of bullets.
- Title as a station plate: blue disc + short title + thin rule. Body in Spectral or Inter, large, few words.
- φ split for two-column slides (61.8 / 38.2), never 50/50.
- A claim carries a number or a chart, never an adjective. One chart per slide, framed, never touched.
- Where a deck makes a case (a talk, a pitch), distill to **three key takeaways** — three, not two, not four.
- One Divine Move across the whole deck: one oversized number, one full-bleed plane, one red line. Not one per slide.
- No transitions, no builds-for-drama, no clip art, no emoji.

### 18.2 Infographic Agent → Instrument mode

- Hairline cell grids. Label every cluster. Right-align numbers.
- Greyscale field; blue for identity; one red spike at the single most important figure.
- The data is the art. No chart junk, no 3D, no gratuitous color. Provenance line at the foot.
- One Move: the one figure that matters, sized to dominate.

### 18.3 Dashboard / Live-Tool Agent → Instrument mode

- Cockpit pattern: vital signs sticky, everything else one decision away.
- Lined-glass feedback; 100ms reactions; the red pulse for live.
- Discs and plates for board identity; the trunk subsystem only if 5+ parallel boards.
- Constraints over warnings. Disable the impossible. No catastrophe in one click.

### 18.4 System-Building Agent → Instrument mode

- Conceptual model first: structure matches the operator's mental model. Domain → Board → Row → Detail.
- Modular, stress-tested components: long names, empty, error, loading, mobile.
- Consistency is the API: same pattern, same meaning, everywhere.
- Build real, not demo. Something working before any presentation.

### 18.5 Document / Report / CV Agent → Editorial mode

- Spectral, reading measure 60–72ch, line-height 1.5–1.6, φ margins.
- Lead each chapter with a vignette or persona, not a thesis sentence.
- Interview snippets as callout boxes, grounded strictly in real data. Every claim backed by data or theory.
- Professional but plain and vivid — readable cover to cover. No academic tone, no jargon. Citations to the highest standard.
- One serif display Move per spread. The rest is calm.

### 18.6 Game / Kit Agent → Play mode

- Physical-first: cards, boards, flashcards, workbooks. Hand-scale, tactile.
- Sato mercy-radius permitted on pieces a human holds.
- Trunk subsystem for piece/family identity; still greyscale-grounded.
- Reward feedback allowed, fast and purposeful. Grid and Golden Section still underneath every layout.

## 19. ACCESSIBILITY — THE LEGIBILITY LAW

Legibility is not a feature. It is the whole promise. A thing you cannot read is a thing that does not work.

- **Contrast:** body text ≥ 4.5:1; large text and UI elements ≥ 3:1 (WCAG AA, the floor). `--ink` on `--paper` passes. Verify white glyphs on `--blue` and `--red` (both pass); verify any red text on paper at small sizes — if it fails, darken or enlarge.
- **Never signal by color alone.** Red also carries a label, a shape, or a position. A red number is also a minus sign. A live dot is also the word "LIVE."
- **Tabular figures** everywhere numbers align or change.
- **Focus is always visible:** the 1px border darken. Never remove focus styling.
- **Tap targets ≥ 44×44px** in any touch context — a place the Sato mercy-radius earns its keep.
- **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`:** kill the pulse and all transitions for users who ask.
- **Text resizes** to 200% without breaking the grid (the Rauschenberg test: the longest name fits at every breakpoint).

## 20. HARD BANS

- ❌ Gradients, drop shadows, glows, blurs, glassmorphism
- ❌ Rounded corners (0px; 2px max) — except the documented Sato mercy-radius
- ❌ A second free accent (one blue identity, one red Move; nothing else)
- ❌ Pure `#000` or pure `#fff`
- ❌ Emoji, decorative icons, illustration, stock imagery
- ❌ Font weights 700+ on data; more than the two sanctioned families
- ❌ Centering dense content; `margin:auto` heroics
- ❌ Bold-everything (hierarchy = size + grey + the contrast gap)
- ❌ Inventing values outside the scales in this manual
- ❌ Filler — placeholder sections, dummy copy, "to balance the layout"
- ❌ A bare colored word/number in content unless it is a data signal (enclosure law)
- ❌ More than one Divine Move per surface
- ❌ A Move you cannot trace to a function (that is decoration)
- ❌ Unicode arrows or chevrons (solid triangle only)
- ❌ Entrance animations, scroll reveals, parallax, decorative loops
- ❌ Tooltips as a substitute for clear affordance
- ❌ Blaming the user for "user errors"
- ❌ Arbitrary spacing not derived from the type scale
- ❌ Loading without feedback
- ❌ Data without provenance
- ❌ Pretension — academic plumage, complexity worn as intelligence, any word that carries no freight

### 22.2 The Checklist (run before shipping anything)

```
□ MODE: Is the human operating, reading, or learning? Mode chosen correctly?
□ FUNCTION: Does every element work? (function buys the right to be seen)
□ SUBTRACTION: What did I remove? If nothing, I haven't finished.
□ TRIPLE LOAD: Does every element inform, move, AND mean? If not, cut it.
□ MoMA LAW: Does every edge resolve to another edge? Nothing floating?
□ GOLDEN SECTION: Is φ in the composition, or did I lazily split in half?
□ THE MOVE: Exactly one bold gesture? Function-compelled? Full size? Inevitable, not daring?
□ PRE-COGNITIVE: Can it be absorbed in one glance? (a sign read twice has failed)
□ LINED GLASS: Is state read instantly, never measured? Feedback under 100ms?
□ COLOR: Grey for normal, blue for identity (enclosed), red for the one exception (bare)?
□ VOICE: Direct, true, economical, unpretentious — and still alive?
□ HONESTY: Provenance, dates, caveats shown? Data untouched?
□ LEGIBILITY: Contrast passes? Not signaling by color alone?
□ INEVITABILITY: Could the user imagine no rational alternative?
```
